Penang Food

My top picks from the food capital of Malaysia!

Char Koay Teow

Stir-fried rice noodles with prawns, cockles, egg, and bean sprouts, often cooked over charcoal for a smoky wok hei flavor.

Asam Laksa

A tangy, spicy fish broth that defines the taste of Penang.

Cendol

Shaved ice with coconut milk, palm sugar (gula melaka), and green rice flour jelly.

Nasi Kandar

Steamed rice served with a variety of curries and side dishes, often eaten at mamak shops.

Hokkien Mee

A prawn-based broth with noodles and pork slices.
